# Business License Guide

**Washington State, Kitsap County, and Bainbridge Island Licensing Requirements**

## 1. OVERVIEW OF LICENSING REQUIREMENTS

### 1.1 Multi-Level Licensing
Operating a web design business on Bainbridge Island requires compliance with:
- **Federal:** EIN registration (see EIN guide)
- **Washington State:** Business license and B&O tax registration
- **Kitsap County:** County business license (if applicable)
- **City of Bainbridge Island:** City business license

### 1.2 Home-Based Business Considerations
**Important:** Home-based businesses have additional zoning and permit requirements. Most residential zones allow home-based professional services with restrictions.

## 2. WASHINGTON STATE REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 State Business License
**Who Needs It:** All businesses operating in Washington State
**Website:** https://bls.dor.wa.gov/
**Cost:** $19 annual base fee + activity fees

**Required Information:**
- Business legal name and structure
- Federal EIN
- Business address
- Description of activities
- Estimated annual gross receipts

### 2.2 Business License Service (BLS) Application

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. **Create Account:** Register at bls.dor.wa.gov
2. **Select Activities:** Choose business activity codes
3. **Complete Application:** Provide all required information
4. **Pay Fees:** Online payment processing
5. **Receive License:** Digital license available immediately

**Primary Activity Code for Web Design:**
- **NAICS 541511:** Custom Computer Programming Services
- **Alternative:** 541512 - Computer Systems Design Services

### 2.3 Washington State Business & Occupation (B&O) Tax

**Registration Required:** All businesses with gross income over $12,000 annually
**Tax Rate:** 1.5% of gross receipts for service businesses
**Reporting:** Monthly, quarterly, or annually based on revenue

**Thresholds:**
- **$0 - $12,000:** No registration required
- **$12,001 - $50,000:** Annual reporting
- **$50,001+:** Quarterly or monthly reporting

**Registration Process:**
- Included with state business license application
- Automatic enrollment when registering business
- First return due by end of month following first month of business

### 2.4 Professional Service Classification
**Note:** Web design generally does not require professional licensing in Washington State. However, if providing related services, additional licenses may be required:

**Potentially Licensed Activities:**
- **Engineering services:** Requires PE license
- **Architectural services:** Requires architect license
- **Legal advice:** Requires law license
- **Accounting services:** May require CPA license

**Safe Activities (No Professional License):**
- Website design and development
- Graphic design
- Digital marketing
- Content management
- Technical consulting

## 3. KITSAP COUNTY REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 County Business License
**Requirement:** Depends on specific business activities and location
**Website:** https://www.kitsapgov.com/dcd/Pages/Business-License.aspx
**Contact:** (360) 337-5777

**Home-Based Business on Bainbridge Island:**
- Most professional services allowed in residential zones
- May require Home Occupation Permit
- Restrictions on signage, employees, and customer visits

### 3.2 Zoning Compliance
**Residential Zones:** Professional services typically allowed with conditions:
- **No employees visiting the home office**
- **Limited client meetings at residence**
- **No external signage**
- **No alterations to residential character**
- **Adequate parking**

### 3.3 Home Occupation Permit
**When Required:**
- Employees working on-site
- Regular client meetings at home
- Significant equipment or modifications
- Commercial vehicles

**Application Process:**
- Contact Kitsap County DCD (Development Services and Code)
- Submit application with site plan
- Pay permit fees ($100-300 typical)
- Schedule inspection if required

## 4. CITY OF BAINBRIDGE ISLAND REQUIREMENTS

### 4.1 City Business License
**Website:** https://www.bainbridgewa.gov/
**Contact:** City Hall (206) 842-2545
**Address:** 280 Madison Avenue N, Bainbridge Island, WA 98110

**Home-Based Business Requirements:**
- **Business License:** Required for all businesses
- **Home Occupation Permit:** May be required
- **Zoning Compliance:** Must comply with residential zone restrictions

### 4.2 Home Occupation Standards
**Allowed by Right (No Permit Required):**
- Professional office use
- No employees on-site
- No customer visits
- No external business signs
- No deliveries except standard mail/packages

**Conditional Use (Permit Required):**
- Client meetings at home office
- One non-resident employee
- Home-based instruction (if applicable)
- Minimal commercial deliveries

### 4.3 Business License Application Process
1. **Contact City Hall:** Confirm current requirements
2. **Submit Application:** Complete business license application
3. **Zoning Review:** City reviews for zoning compliance
4. **Pay Fees:** License fees (typically $50-150 annually)
5. **Receive License:** Business license certificate

### 4.4 Signage Restrictions
**Residential Areas:**
- **No external business signs** in most residential zones
- **Window signs:** May be prohibited or restricted
- **Vehicle signage:** Personal vehicles generally allowed

### 5.1 EIN Registration
- See separate EIN guide

### 5.2 Business Structure Registration

- Must be completed before business licensing

## 6. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR AGE 18 TRANSITION

### 6.1 Current Situation (Age 17)
**Cannot file as business owner until age 18:**
- State business license requires 18+ years
- City business license requires 18+ years
- All licensing must wait until legal age

### 6.2 Transition Timeline (Age 18)
**Day 1:** File LLC formation
**Day 2-3:** Apply for EIN
**Week 1:** Apply for state business license
**Week 2:** Apply for county permits (if needed)
**Week 3:** Apply for city business license
**Week 4:** Begin business operations

### 6.3 Parent/Guardian Considerations
**Temporary Options:**
- Operate as sole proprietor under parent supervision
- Parent files business license temporarily
- Transfer ownership at age 18

**NOT RECOMMENDED:** Creates tax and liability complications

## 7. COST SUMMARY

### 7.1 State Fees
- **Business License:** $19 base + activity fees ($5-25 typical)
- **Annual Total:** $25-50

### 7.2 County Fees (If Required)
- **Home Occupation Permit:** $100-300 (if needed)
- **Zoning Review:** $50-100

### 7.3 City Fees
- **Business License:** $50-150 annually
- **Home Occupation Permit:** $75-200 (if needed)

### 7.4 Total First-Year Estimate
**Minimum (Simple Home Office):** $75-200
**With Permits:** $300-650
**Annual Ongoing:** $75-200

## 8. COMPLIANCE CALENDAR

### 8.1 Annual Requirements
- **State Business License Renewal:** Due on business anniversary date
- **City Business License Renewal:** Due January 31st typically
- **B&O Tax Returns:** Due dates vary by filing frequency

### 8.2 Ongoing Obligations
- **B&O Tax Payments:** Monthly, quarterly, or annually
- **License Display:** Keep current licenses available for inspection
- **Address Changes:** Update all licenses within 30 days
- **Activity Changes:** Update licenses when services change

## 9. RECORD KEEPING

### 9.1 Required Documentation
- All business licenses (state, county, city)
- Permit applications and approvals
- Fee payment receipts
- Correspondence with licensing agencies
- Zoning compliance documentation

### 9.2 Display Requirements
- **State License:** Available upon request
- **City License:** May need to be displayed at business location
- **Permits:** Keep on file and available for inspection

## 10. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

### 10.1 State Penalties
- **Unlicensed Business:** $300 penalty + license fees
- **Late B&O Tax:** 5% penalty + 1% monthly interest
- **Failure to File:** Additional penalties and potential closure

### 10.2 Local Penalties
- **Unlicensed Operation:** Fines $100-500 per day
- **Zoning Violations:** Stop work orders and fines
- **Home Occupation Violations:** $100-250 per day
