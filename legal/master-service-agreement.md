# Master Service Agreement

**WEBSITE DESIGN AND DEVELOPMENT SERVICES**

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date") between Amotive Design LLC, a Washington limited liability company ("Company" or "Amotive"), and [CLIENT LEGAL NAME] ("Client").

## 1. SERVICES AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Services Provided
Company agrees to provide website design and development services as specified in individual Statements of Work ("SOW") or project proposals. Standard services include:

- Custom website design and development
- Content management system integration
- Basic search engine optimization setup
- Mobile-responsive design
- SSL certificate installation and configuration
- Basic website maintenance and support

### 1.2 Scope Limitations
Services are limited to those specifically outlined in the applicable SOW. Additional work requires a separate SOW or written change order.

### 1.3 Client Content Requirements
Client must provide all required content, images, copy, and materials within thirty (30) days of project commencement. Delays in content provision will extend project timeline accordingly.

## 2. PAYMENT TERMS

### 2.1 Project Fee Structure
**Total Project Fee: $1,500** (baseline pricing)

Payment structure:
- **50% Non-Refundable Retainer:** Due upon signing this Agreement
- **50% Final Payment:** Due upon project completion and delivery

### 2.2 Payment Schedule
- Retainer: $750 due within 5 business days of Agreement execution
- Final Payment: $750 due within 10 business days of project completion notice
- Late payments incur 1.5% monthly service charge (18% annually)

### 2.3 Additional Costs
- Change orders: $200 per request (minimum)
- Rush delivery (less than standard timeline): 50% surcharge
- Domain registration/renewal: Pass-through cost + $25 admin fee
- Premium hosting: Pass-through cost + 15% markup
- Third-party licenses/subscriptions: Pass-through cost + 10% markup

## 3. TIMELINE AND DELIVERABLES

### 3.1 Standard Timeline
**Five (5) business days** from receipt of all required content and materials.

### 3.2 Project Phases
1. Design mockup delivery: 2 business days
2. Revision round: 1 business day
3. Final development: 2 business days
4. Final delivery: Same day upon approval

### 3.3 Revision Policy
Client is entitled to **two (2) rounds of revisions** included in base fee. Additional revisions constitute change orders at $200 each.

### 3.4 Force Majeure
Company shall not be liable for delays caused by circumstances beyond reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, war, terrorism, pandemic, government regulations, natural disasters, or internet service disruptions.

## 4. DOMAIN AND HOSTING OWNERSHIP

### 4.1 Domain Ownership
**Company retains ownership** of all domains purchased on Client's behalf until final payment is received and transfer fee is paid.

### 4.2 Hosting Ownership
**Company retains ownership** of hosting accounts and configurations. Client receives usage rights during the term of service.

### 4.3 Transfer Policy
Upon request and payment of **$500 transfer fee**, Company will:
- Transfer domain registration to Client's account
- Provide hosting migration assistance
- Deliver all source files and databases
- Provide 30 days transition support

Transfer fee is non-refundable and due in advance of transfer initiation.

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 5.1 Client Content License
Client grants Company a limited, non-exclusive license to use Client's content, trademarks, and materials solely for providing Services under this Agreement.

### 5.2 Developed Work
Upon final payment, Company grants Client a non-exclusive license to use the developed website. Company retains ownership of underlying code, frameworks, and proprietary methodologies.

### 5.3 Third-Party Components
Website may incorporate third-party components, plugins, or frameworks subject to their respective licenses. Client is responsible for compliance with third-party license terms.

### 5.4 Portfolio Rights
Company retains the right to display completed work in portfolios, marketing materials, and case studies unless Client specifically requests confidentiality in writing.

## 6. WARRANTIES AND DISCLAIMERS

### 6.1 Limited Warranty
Company warrants that delivered website will be free from material defects for thirty (30) days following delivery. This warranty covers only the initial delivered work and does not extend to:

- Third-party plugins or components
- Client-made modifications
- Hosting environment issues
- Domain or DNS problems

### 6.2 ADA COMPLIANCE DISCLAIMER
**IMPORTANT:** Company does not warrant compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) or Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). Client is solely responsible for ensuring website accessibility compliance. ADA compliance services available as separate engagement.

### 6.3 SEO DISCLAIMER
Company makes **NO GUARANTEE** regarding search engine rankings, organic traffic increases, or SEO performance. SEO services are provided on a best-efforts basis only.

### 6.4 Performance Disclaimer
Website performance depends on hosting environment, content optimization, and third-party services. Company does not guarantee specific load times or uptime percentages.

## 7. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

### 7.1 Liability Cap
Company's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total amount paid by Client for the specific project giving rise to the claim.

### 7.2 Consequential Damages
IN NO EVENT SHALL COMPANY BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOST PROFITS, BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, OR LOSS OF DATA.

### 7.3 Client Indemnification
Client agrees to indemnify and hold Company harmless from any claims arising from:
- Client's use of the delivered website
- Client-provided content or materials
- Client's violation of third-party rights
- Client's non-compliance with applicable laws

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 8.1 Mutual Confidentiality
Both parties agree to maintain confidentiality of proprietary information disclosed during the engagement. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.

### 8.2 Excluded Information
Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:
- Is publicly available
- Was known prior to disclosure
- Is independently developed
- Must be disclosed by law

## 9. TERMINATION

### 9.1 Termination for Convenience
Either party may terminate with thirty (30) days written notice. Upon termination:
- Client remains liable for all work completed
- Company will deliver work-in-progress
- No refund of retainer fee

### 9.2 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate immediately for material breach that remains uncured after ten (10) days written notice.

### 9.3 Effect of Termination
Upon termination, all payment obligations become immediately due, and Company's license to use Client content terminates.

## 10. NON-SOLICITATION

### 10.1 Client Restriction
For twelve (12) months following Agreement termination, Client agrees not to directly solicit or hire Company's employees or contractors who worked on Client's project.

### 10.2 Remedy
Violation of non-solicitation results in liquidated damages of $10,000 per individual solicited, plus Company's attorney fees and costs.

## 11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 11.1 Binding Arbitration
Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration in **Kitsap County, Washington** under the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the American Arbitration Association.

### 11.2 Attorney Fees
Prevailing party in arbitration shall be entitled to reasonable attorney fees and costs.

### 11.3 Injunctive Relief
Either party may seek injunctive relief in court for breach of confidentiality or intellectual property provisions.

## 12. ELECTRONIC SIGNATURES

### 12.1 E-SIGN Compliance
This Agreement may be executed electronically in compliance with the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (E-SIGN Act). Electronic signatures shall have the same legal effect as handwritten signatures.

### 12.2 Record Retention
Company will maintain electronic records of signed agreements for a minimum of seven (7) years.

## 13. NON-DISPARAGEMENT

### 13.1 Mutual Non-Disparagement
Neither party shall make any public statements or communications that disparage, criticize, or harm the reputation of the other party.

### 13.2 Permitted Communications
This provision does not restrict factual statements required by law or truthful responses to direct inquiries about work quality.

## 14. SURVIVAL CLAUSE

### 14.1 Surviving Provisions
The following provisions survive termination of this Agreement:
- Payment obligations
- Intellectual property provisions
- Confidentiality obligations
- Liability limitations
- Indemnification obligations
- Non-solicitation restrictions
- Non-disparagement obligations

### 15.2 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.

### 15.3 Amendment
