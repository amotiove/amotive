# Tax Compliance Guide

**WASHINGTON STATE AND FEDERAL TAX REQUIREMENTS FOR AMOTIVE DESIGN LLC**

---

## 1. WASHINGTON STATE TAXES

### 1.1 Business & Occupation (B&O) Tax

**What is B&O Tax?**
Washington State's primary business tax based on gross receipts, not profit. All businesses with gross income over $12,000 annually must register and file returns.

**Tax Classification for Web Design:**
- **Primary:** Service and Other Activities
- **Tax Rate:** 1.5% of gross receipts
- **Alternative:** Professional Services (if qualifying criteria met)

**Registration Requirements:**
- **Threshold:** $12,000+ annual gross receipts
- **Registration:** Within 30 days of exceeding threshold
- **Website:** bls.dor.wa.gov (Business License Service)

**B&O Tax Calculation Example:**
```
Annual Revenue: $50,000
Tax Rate: 1.5%
Annual B&O Tax: $750
Quarterly Payment: $187.50
```

### 1.2 B&O Tax Filing Schedule

**Filing Frequency Based on Tax Liability:**

**Annual Filing:**
- **Tax Liability:** Less than $480 per year
- **Due Date:** January 31st of following year
- **Payment:** Due with return

**Quarterly Filing:**
- **Tax Liability:** $480+ but less than $4,800 per year
- **Due Dates:** April 30, July 31, October 31, January 31
- **Payment:** Due with each quarterly return

**Monthly Filing:**
- **Tax Liability:** $4,800+ per year
- **Due Date:** 25th of following month
- **Payment:** Due with monthly return

### 1.3 Washington State Sales Tax

**Digital Services Classification:**
Washington State generally does NOT tax digital services like:
- Website design and development
- Digital consulting
- Software as a Service (SaaS)
- Digital marketing services

**Taxable Items (if applicable):**
- Physical products sold to customers
- Software delivered on tangible media
- Digital products sold to end consumers (varies)

**Sales Tax Registration:**
- Only required if selling taxable goods/services
- Register through Business License Service
- Current rate: 6.5% state + local rates (varies by location)

### 1.4 B&O Tax Deductions and Credits

**Small Business B&O Credit:**
- Credit up to $70 per month ($840 annually)
- Phases out as gross receipts increase
- Automatic when filing returns

**High Technology B&O Credit:**
- May apply to software development activities
- Requires specific qualifying criteria
- Consult tax professional for eligibility

**Manufacturing B&O Rate:**
- Lower rate (0.484%) for qualifying manufacturing
- Unlikely to apply to typical web design services
- Custom software development may qualify

## 2. FEDERAL TAXES

### 2.1 Income Tax Structure

**LLC Tax Election Options:**

**Default (Disregarded Entity):**
- Single-member LLC taxed as sole proprietorship
- Income reported on Form 1040, Schedule C
- Self-employment tax applies to net profit
- No separate business tax return required

**S-Corporation Election:**
- File Form 2553 within 2 months 15 days of tax year
- Separate business tax return (Form 1120S)
- Owner must take reasonable salary (subject to payroll taxes)
- Distributions above salary not subject to self-employment tax
- Potential tax savings on self-employment tax

### 2.2 Self-Employment Tax

**Rate:** 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)
**Applied To:** Net profit from Schedule C
**Income Limit:** Social Security portion caps at $160,200 (2023)

**Self-Employment Tax Example:**
```
Net Business Profit: $40,000
Self-Employment Tax: $5,652 (40,000 × 92.35% × 15.3%)
Income Tax: Based on total income and deductions
Total Tax Burden: Self-employment tax + income tax
```

### 2.3 Quarterly Estimated Tax Payments

**Who Must Pay:**
- Expect to owe $1,000+ in tax
- Previous year tax liability over $1,000

**Due Dates:**
- **Q1:** April 15
- **Q2:** June 15
- **Q3:** September 15
- **Q4:** January 15 (following year)

**Calculation Method:**
- 100% of prior year tax (safe harbor)
- 90% of current year tax
- Whichever is smaller

**Estimated Tax Worksheet:**
```
Expected Annual Net Profit: $40,000
Estimated Self-Employment Tax: $5,652
Estimated Income Tax: $4,000
Total Estimated Tax: $9,652
Quarterly Payment: $2,413
```

## 4. TAX PLANNING STRATEGIES

### 4.1 Business Structure Optimization

**Sole Proprietorship/LLC Benefits:**
- Simplified tax filing
- Lower compliance costs
- No double taxation
- Direct deduction of business losses

**S-Corporation Benefits:**
- Potential self-employment tax savings
- Clear employee vs. owner distinction
- Retirement plan options
- Potentially better sale structure

**Break-Even Analysis for S-Corp:**
```
Net Profit Required: ~$60,000+
Self-Employment Tax Savings: ~$4,000-6,000
Additional Costs: Payroll taxes, accounting, compliance
Net Benefit: Savings minus additional costs
```

### 4.2 Income and Expense Timing

**Year-End Strategies:**
- **Accelerate Expenses:** Purchase equipment in December
- **Defer Income:** Delay December invoicing to January
- **Software Subscriptions:** Pay annually in December
- **Professional Services:** Prepay legal/accounting fees

**Equipment Purchases:**
- **Section 179 Deduction:** Up to $1,160,000 (2023)
- **Bonus Depreciation:** 80% first-year depreciation (2023)
- **Regular Depreciation:** 5-7 year schedule for computers

### 4.3 Retirement Planning

**SEP-IRA for Self-Employed:**
- Contribute up to 25% of net self-employment income
- Maximum contribution: $66,000 (2023)
- Tax-deductible contributions
- Easy setup and maintenance

**Solo 401(k):**
- Employee contribution: Up to $22,500 (2023)
- Employer contribution: Up to 25% of compensation
- Total contribution: Up to $66,000 (2023)
- More complex but higher contribution limits

## 6. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### 6.1 Washington State Issues
- **Missing B&O Registration:** Penalties for late registration
- **Incorrect Tax Classification:** Using wrong business activity code
- **Late Filing:** Accumulating penalties and interest
- **Mixing Personal and Business:** Poor record keeping

### 6.2 Federal Issues
- **Estimated Tax Underpayment:** Penalties for insufficient payments
- **Self-Employment Tax Surprise:** Not setting aside adequate funds
- **Poor Record Keeping:** Missing deductions and IRS issues
- **Home Office Deduction Errors:** Improper calculations
