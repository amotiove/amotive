# Independent Contractor Agreement

**PROFESSIONAL SERVICES CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT**

This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date") between Amotive Design LLC, a Washington limited liability company ("Company" or "Amotive"), and [CONTRACTOR NAME] ("Contractor").

## 1. ENGAGEMENT AND SERVICES

### 1.1 Independent Contractor Relationship
Contractor agrees to provide services to Company as an independent contractor, not as an employee. This relationship does not create any employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

### 1.2 Services to be Provided
Contractor will provide the following services ("Services") as specified in individual Statements of Work ("SOW"):

**Typical Services May Include:**
- Website design and development
- Graphic design and branding
- Content creation and copywriting
- SEO and digital marketing
- Technical consulting and support
- Project management and coordination

### 1.3 Statement of Work
Specific projects will be defined in separate SOWs that include:
- Detailed scope of work
- Deliverables and specifications
- Timeline and milestones
- Compensation and payment terms
- Any project-specific terms

## 2. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

### 2.1 Payment Structure
Contractor compensation will be specified in each SOW and may be:
- Fixed fee per project
- Hourly rate
- Performance-based compensation
- Revenue sharing (for specific arrangements)

### 2.2 Payment Terms
- **Invoicing:** Contractor submits invoices monthly or upon project completion
- **Payment:** Company pays within 30 days of invoice receipt
- **Late Payments:** 1.5% monthly service charge on overdue amounts
- **Expenses:** Pre-approved expenses reimbursed with receipts

### 2.3 Tax Responsibilities
Contractor is responsible for:
- All federal, state, and local taxes
- Self-employment tax
- Quarterly estimated tax payments
- Form 1099 reporting compliance

Company will issue Form 1099-NEC if payments exceed $600 annually.

## 3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT

### 3.1 Work Product Ownership
**ALL work product, ideas, inventions, and deliverables created by Contractor in connection with Services ("Work Product") shall be the exclusive property of Company.**

Work Product includes but is not limited to:
- Code, scripts, and software
- Design files and graphics
- Content and copy
- Documentation and processes
- Ideas, concepts, and methodologies developed during engagement

### 3.2 Assignment of Rights
Contractor hereby assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in Work Product, including:
- Copyrights and moral rights
- Patent rights
- Trade secret rights
- Any other intellectual property rights

### 3.3 Work Made for Hire
To the extent legally possible, Work Product shall be considered "work made for hire" under copyright law. If any Work Product does not qualify as work made for hire, this Agreement serves as an assignment of all rights to Company.

### 3.4 Contractor's Pre-Existing IP
Contractor retains ownership of pre-existing intellectual property. If pre-existing IP is incorporated into Work Product:
- Contractor grants Company a perpetual, royalty-free license to use
- Contractor must identify pre-existing IP before incorporation
- Company may require separate licensing agreement

### 3.5 Third-Party Materials
Contractor represents that Work Product will not infringe third-party intellectual property rights. If third-party materials are used:
- Contractor must obtain proper licenses
- Contractor must document all third-party materials
- Company must approve use before incorporation

## 4. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 4.1 Confidential Information Definition
"Confidential Information" includes any non-public information disclosed by either party, including:
- Business strategies and plans
- Client lists and information
- Financial information
- Technical specifications and processes
- Marketing plans and strategies
- Any information marked as confidential

### 4.2 Confidentiality Obligations
Contractor agrees to:
- Maintain strict confidentiality of all Confidential Information
- Use Confidential Information solely for performing Services
- Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties
- Return or destroy Confidential Information upon termination

### 4.3 Excluded Information
Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:
- Is or becomes publicly available through no breach
- Was known prior to disclosure
- Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
- Must be disclosed by law (with notice to disclosing party)

### 4.4 Survival
Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for five (5) years.

## 5. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

### 5.1 Non-Competition
During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months thereafter, Contractor agrees not to:
- Directly compete with Company in the same geographic market
- Provide substantially similar services to Company's clients
- Use Company's Confidential Information to compete

**Geographic Scope:** Pacific Northwest region (Washington, Oregon, Northern California)
**Service Scope:** Website design and development services

### 5.2 Client Non-Solicitation
For eighteen (18) months following termination, Contractor agrees not to:
- Solicit business from Company's clients
- Accept work from clients Contractor worked with through Company
- Induce clients to terminate or reduce business with Company

### 5.3 Employee Non-Solicitation
For twelve (12) months following termination, Contractor agrees not to:
- Recruit or hire Company employees or contractors
- Induce Company personnel to terminate their relationship
- Solicit Company personnel for any business purpose

### 5.4 Reasonable Restrictions
The parties acknowledge that these restrictions are reasonable and necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests. If any restriction is deemed unenforceable, it shall be modified to the maximum enforceable extent.

### 5.5 Liquidated Damages
Violation of non-compete or non-solicitation provisions shall result in liquidated damages of $10,000 per violation, plus Company's attorney fees and costs.

## 6. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

### 6.1 No Employee Benefits
Contractor is not entitled to:
- Health insurance or other benefits
- Workers' compensation coverage
- Unemployment benefits
- Paid time off or holidays
- Retirement or pension benefits

### 6.2 Contractor Responsibilities
Contractor shall:
- Provide own equipment and workspace
- Set own work schedule (subject to project deadlines)
- Use own methods and processes
- Maintain required licenses and certifications
- Carry appropriate insurance coverage

### 6.3 No Exclusivity
Contractor may work for other clients during this engagement, provided:
- No conflicts of interest arise
- Confidentiality obligations are maintained
- Service quality and timeliness are not compromised
- No competing services are provided to direct competitors

## 7. WARRANTIES AND REPRESENTATIONS

### 7.1 Contractor Warranties
Contractor represents and warrants:
- Full authority to enter this Agreement
- Services will be performed in professional, workmanlike manner
- Work Product will be original and not infringe third-party rights
- Compliance with all applicable laws and regulations
- Maintenance of required licenses and qualifications

### 7.2 Company Warranties
Company represents and warrants:
- Full authority to enter this Agreement
- Timely payment for satisfactory Services
- Provision of necessary information and resources

### 7.3 Disclaimer
EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH HEREIN, ALL SERVICES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS" WITHOUT WARRANTIES OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED.

## 8. INDEMNIFICATION

### 8.1 Contractor Indemnification
Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold Company harmless from claims arising from:
- Contractor's negligence or willful misconduct
- Infringement of third-party intellectual property rights
- Violation of applicable laws or regulations
- Breach of this Agreement

### 8.2 Company Indemnification
Company agrees to indemnify Contractor from claims arising from:
- Company's use of Work Product as authorized
- Company's negligence or willful misconduct
- Company-provided materials or specifications

## 9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 9.1 Liability Cap
Each party's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total amount paid or payable to Contractor under the applicable SOW.

### 9.2 Consequential Damages
NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS.

## 10. TERMINATION

### 10.1 Termination for Convenience
Either party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW:
- With thirty (30) days written notice
- Immediately upon completion of all SOWs
- By mutual written agreement

### 10.2 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate immediately for:
- Material breach that remains uncured after ten (10) days written notice
- Insolvency or bankruptcy
- Violation of confidentiality or intellectual property provisions

### 10.3 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:
- Contractor completes work-in-progress if feasible
- Company pays for work satisfactorily completed
- Contractor returns all Company property and Confidential Information
- All obligations survive that are intended to survive

## 11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 11.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Washington without regard to conflict of law principles.

### 11.2 Dispute Resolution Process
Disputes shall be resolved as follows:
1. **Direct Negotiation:** 30 days good faith negotiation
2. **Mediation:** Non-binding mediation in Kitsap County, WA
3. **Arbitration:** Binding arbitration under AAA Commercial Rules

### 11.3 Attorney Fees
Prevailing party in any legal proceeding shall be entitled to reasonable attorney fees and costs.

## 12. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 12.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement and any SOWs constitute the entire agreement between the parties and supersede all prior negotiations and agreements.

### 12.2 Amendment
This Agreement may only be amended by written document signed by both parties.

### 12.3 Assignment
Contractor may not assign this Agreement without Company's written consent. Company may assign to affiliates or successors.

### 12.4 Severability
If any provision is found unenforceable, the remainder shall remain in full force and effect.

### 12.5 Force Majeure
Neither party shall be liable for performance delays caused by circumstances beyond reasonable control.

### 12.6 Notices
All notices must be in writing and delivered to the addresses set forth below or as updated in writing.

### 12.7 Survival
The following provisions survive termination:
- Intellectual Property Assignment
- Confidentiality
- Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation
- Indemnification
- Limitation of Liability
- Dispute Resolution
