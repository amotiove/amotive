## USAGE GUIDELINES

### When to Send Cease and Desist Letters

**PAYMENT ISSUES:**
- After 60+ days past due
- Multiple collection attempts failed
- Clear ability to pay exists
- Relationship is beyond repair

**IP INFRINGEMENT:**
- Clear evidence of unauthorized use
- Substantial similarity to original work
- Commercial use or competitive advantage
- Documented ownership of IP

**CONTRACT VIOLATIONS:**
- Material breach of agreement terms
- Ongoing violation despite warnings
- Substantial damages occurring
- Clear evidence of intentional breach

### Legal Considerations

**Before Sending:**
- [ ] Verify all facts and documentation
- [ ] Review contract terms and applicable law
- [ ] Calculate damages and legal costs
- [ ] Consider alternative resolution methods
- [ ] Ensure compliance with debt collection laws

**Documentation Required:**
- [ ] Original contracts and agreements
- [ ] Invoice and payment records
- [ ] Communication history
- [ ] Evidence of infringement/breach
- [ ] Damage calculations

**Follow-Up Actions:**
- [ ] Send by certified mail, return receipt requested
- [ ] Keep detailed records of all communications
- [ ] Set calendar reminders for response deadlines
- [ ] Prepare for potential legal action
- [ ] Consider settlement negotiations

### Professional Standards

**Tone and Language:**
- Professional but firm
- Factual, not emotional
- Clear demands and deadlines
- Legal consequences clearly stated
- Opportunity for resolution provided

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Threatening illegal actions
- Making false claims
- Unclear or unreasonable demands
- Failure to follow up
- Poor documentation

**[ATTORNEY REVIEW RECOMMENDED]** - Have all cease and desist letters reviewed by an attorney before sending to ensure legal compliance and maximum effectiveness.
