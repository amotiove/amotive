# Insurance Guide for Web Design Business

**Essential Insurance Coverage for Amotive Design LLC**

## 1. ERRORS & OMISSIONS (E&O) INSURANCE

### 1.1 What is E&O Insurance?
Professional Liability Insurance (E&O) protects against claims of:
- Professional mistakes or negligence
- Failure to deliver services as promised
- Data breaches or security incidents
- Intellectual property infringement claims
- Missed deadlines causing client losses

### 1.2 Why You Need It
**High-Risk Scenarios for Web Designers:**
- Website launches with broken functionality
- Security vulnerabilities leading to client data breach
- Copyright infringement claims on images or content
- Website downtime causing business losses
- ADA compliance failures
- SEO promises not met
- Project delays causing missed product launches

### 1.3 Recommended Coverage Amounts

**Minimum Coverage:**
- **Per Claim:** $1,000,000
- **Annual Aggregate:** $1,000,000
- **Cyber Liability:** $500,000

**Preferred Coverage:**
- **Per Claim:** $2,000,000
- **Annual Aggregate:** $2,000,000
- **Cyber Liability:** $1,000,000

**Premium Protection:** Consider coverage for lawsuit defense costs even if you win.

## 3. CYBER LIABILITY INSURANCE

### 3.1 Why Separate Cyber Coverage?
E&O may not cover all cyber incidents:
- **Data Breaches:** Client personal information exposed
- **Ransomware Attacks:** Business interruption and recovery costs
- **Network Failures:** Third-party damages from outages
- **Regulatory Fines:** GDPR, CCPA compliance violations

### 3.2 Recommended Cyber Coverage
- **Data Breach Response:** $500,000 minimum
- **Business Interruption:** 30-60 days coverage
- **Regulatory Defense:** $250,000 minimum
- **Cyber Extortion:** $100,000 minimum

## 4. GENERAL LIABILITY INSURANCE

### 4.1 What It Covers
- **Bodily Injury:** Slip and fall at your office/client site
- **Property Damage:** Accidentally damaging client property
- **Personal Injury:** Libel, slander, copyright infringement
- **Advertising Injury:** Claims related to marketing materials

### 4.2 Recommended Coverage
- **Per Occurrence:** $1,000,000
- **Annual Aggregate:** $2,000,000
- **Monthly Premium:** $20-40

### 4.3 When You Need It
- Meeting clients at their offices
- Having clients visit your workspace
- Attending networking events
- Any physical business activities

## 5. BUSINESS PERSONAL PROPERTY INSURANCE

### 5.1 What It Covers
- Computer equipment and hardware
- Software licenses
- Office furniture and equipment
- Business records and data

### 5.2 Home Office Considerations
**Important:** Standard homeowner's/renter's insurance typically excludes business property. You need:
- Business property endorsement on homeowner's policy, OR
- Separate business personal property insurance

### 5.3 Recommended Coverage
- **Equipment Value:** Full replacement cost
- **Off-Premises Coverage:** For equipment taken to client sites
- **Data Recovery:** Cost to recreate lost business data

## 7. SHOPPING CHECKLIST

### 7.1 Before Getting Quotes
- [ ] Calculate total equipment value
- [ ] Estimate annual revenue
- [ ] List all services you provide
- [ ] Identify biggest risk exposures
- [ ] Research any claims history

### 7.2 Questions to Ask Insurers
- **Coverage:** What specific activities are covered?
- **Exclusions:** What is NOT covered?
- **Deductibles:** Per claim vs. annual deductibles?
- **Defense Costs:** Are legal fees included or separate?
- **Territory:** Coverage for remote work/travel?
- **Limits:** Per claim vs. aggregate limits?
- **Cyber:** Is cyber liability included or separate?

### 7.3 Quote Comparison Spreadsheet
Create a comparison with:
- Provider name
- Monthly/annual premium
- Coverage limits
- Deductibles
- Key exclusions
- Additional benefits

## 8. COMMON EXCLUSIONS TO WATCH FOR

### 8.1 Professional Liability Exclusions
- **Prior Acts:** Claims for work done before coverage started
- **Criminal Acts:** Intentional wrongdoing
- **Employment Issues:** Hiring/firing disputes
- **Contractual Liability:** Claims arising from specific contract terms
- **Warranty Claims:** Guarantees or warranties you provide

### 8.2 Cyber Liability Exclusions
- **War/Terrorism:** Nation-state cyber attacks
- **Infrastructure Failure:** Internet backbone failures
- **Prior Knowledge:** Known security vulnerabilities
- **Employee Theft:** Intentional employee actions

## 9. AGE CONSIDERATIONS (17 → 18 TRANSITION)

### 9.1 Current Situation (Age 17)
- Insurance policies typically require business owner to be 18+
- Parent/guardian may need to be named as additional insured
- May need separate business personal property coverage under parent's homeowner's policy

### 9.2 Transition at Age 18
- [ ] Transfer policy to your name
- [ ] Update business registration information
- [ ] Ensure continuous coverage (no gaps)
- [ ] Review coverage limits based on business growth

## 10. CLAIM PREVENTION BEST PRACTICES

### 10.1 Documentation
- **Contracts:** Clear scope and deliverables
- **Communications:** Email trails of all decisions
- **Testing:** Document website testing procedures
- **Backups:** Regular backups and recovery testing

### 10.2 Risk Management
- **Client Agreements:** Strong limitation of liability clauses
- **Security:** Regular security updates and monitoring
- **Training:** Stay current with web development best practices
- **Subcontractors:** Ensure they have their own insurance

### 10.3 Incident Response Plan
1. **Immediate:** Secure systems and preserve evidence
2. **Notify:** Contact insurance carrier within 24-48 hours
3. **Document:** Gather all relevant information
4. **Cooperate:** Work with insurance company investigation
5. **Legal:** Don't admit fault without carrier approval
