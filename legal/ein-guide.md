# EIN Registration Guide

**Federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) for Amotive Design LLC**

## 1. WHAT IS AN EIN?

### 1.1 Definition
An Employer Identification Number (EIN), also known as a Federal Tax Identification Number, is a unique nine-digit number assigned by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to identify a business entity.

### 1.2 Format
EIN format: **XX-XXXXXXX** (Example: 12-3456789)

### 1.3 Why You Need an EIN

**Required For:**
- Opening business bank accounts
- Filing tax returns
- Hiring employees (even part-time)
- Setting up business credit
- Working with clients who need tax forms
- Setting up payment processors (Stripe, PayPal, etc.)
- Professional credibility

**Even if you don't have employees**, an EIN is strongly recommended for LLCs to:
- Separate personal and business finances
- Protect personal SSN from exposure
- Prepare for future growth

## 2. ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Who Can Apply
- **LLC Members/Managers:** Must be 18+ years old
- **Responsible Party:** Person with control over entity
- **Valid SSN or ITIN:** Required for application

### 2.2 Age Considerations (17 → 18 Transition)
**If currently 17:**
- Cannot apply for EIN until 18th birthday
- LLC formation must wait until age 18 (WA State requirement)
- Parent/guardian cannot apply on your behalf for your LLC

**On 18th birthday:**
- Apply for EIN immediately after LLC formation
- Use your SSN as the responsible party identifier

## 3. STEP-BY-STEP APPLICATION PROCESS

### 3.1 Preparation (Before Applying)
**Required Information:**
- Your legal name and SSN
- LLC legal name (exact match with WA State filing)
- LLC formation date
- WA State LLC filing number
- Business address (can be home office)
- Mailing address (if different)
- Reason for applying (starting new business)
- Type of entity (LLC)
- Number of LLC members
- Business activity description

### 3.2 Online Application (Recommended)
**Website:** https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online

**Process:**
1. Click "Apply Online Now"
2. Select "LLC" as entity type
3. Enter responsible party information (you)
4. Enter LLC information
5. Provide business details
6. Review and submit
7. Receive EIN immediately

**Hours:** Monday-Friday, 7 AM - 10 PM ET
**Processing Time:** Immediate
**Cost:** FREE

### 3.3 Alternative Application Methods

**By Fax:**
- Form SS-4 to: (855) 641-6935
- Processing time: 4 business days
- Business hours: Monday-Friday

**By Mail:**
- Form SS-4 to: Internal Revenue Service, Attn: EIN Operation, Cincinnati, OH 45999
- Processing time: 4-5 weeks
- Include return envelope

**By Phone:**
- International applicants only
- (267) 941-1099 (not toll-free)

## 4. FORM SS-4 COMPLETION GUIDE

### 4.1 Key Fields

**Line 1:** Legal name of entity
- Enter: "Amotive Design LLC" (exact match with state filing)

**Line 2:** Trade name (if different)
- Leave blank if operating under LLC name
- Enter "Amotive" if using shortened name

**Line 3:** Executor, administrator, trustee, "care of" name
- Leave blank for standard LLC

**Lines 4a-4b:** Mailing address
- Enter your business address (can be home office)

**Lines 5a-5b:** Street address (if different from mailing)
- Enter if different from mailing address

**Line 6:** County and state where principal business is located
- County: Kitsap County (if Bainbridge Island)
- State: Washington

**Line 7a:** Name of responsible party
- Enter: [Your full legal name]

**Line 7b:** SSN, ITIN, or EIN of responsible party
- Enter: Your Social Security Number

**Line 8a:** Is this application for a limited liability company (LLC)?
- Check: Yes

**Line 8b:** If 8a is "Yes," enter number of LLC members
- Enter: 1 (single-member LLC)

**Line 9a:** Type of entity
- Check: "Other" and write "LLC"

**Line 10:** Reason for applying
- Check: "Started new business"

**Line 11:** Date business started or acquired
- Enter: Date you plan to start operations (can be future date)

**Line 12:** Closing month of accounting year
- Enter: "December" (recommended for calendar year)

**Line 13:** Highest number of employees expected in next 12 months
- Enter: 0 (if no employees planned)

**Line 14:** Check one box for principal activity
- Check: "Other" and describe: "Website Design and Development Services"

**Line 15:** Indicate principal line of merchandise sold
- Enter: "Website Design and Development Services"

**Line 16:** Has the applicant entity shown on line 1 ever applied for an EIN?
- Check: No (for new LLC)

**Line 17-18:** Third party designee (optional)
- Leave blank unless using an accountant or attorney

### 4.2 Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Mismatched LLC name:** Must exactly match state registration
- **Wrong entity type:** Must select LLC, not sole proprietorship
- **Incomplete responsible party info:** Must include full SSN
- **Future start date too far out:** Keep within reasonable timeframe
- **Inconsistent addresses:** Use same address format throughout

## 5. AFTER RECEIVING YOUR EIN

### 5.1 EIN Confirmation Letter
- Save the official EIN assignment notice
- Print multiple copies for your records
- Store electronically and in physical files
- You'll need this for banking and other applications

### 5.2 Immediate Next Steps
1. **Update business records:** Add EIN to all documentation
2. **Open business bank account:** Schedule within 1-2 weeks
3. **Update WA State registration:** Add EIN to state records if required
4. **Notify accounting software:** Set up business accounting
5. **Update invoicing:** Add EIN to invoice templates

### 5.3 Banking Requirements
Most banks require for business account opening:
- EIN confirmation letter
- LLC Certificate of Formation
- Operating Agreement
- Valid ID
- Initial deposit ($100-500 typical minimum)

## 6. TAX IMPLICATIONS

### 6.1 Default Tax Treatment
**Single-Member LLC:** Default is "disregarded entity"
- Taxes filed on personal return (Form 1040, Schedule C)
- No separate business tax return required
- Self-employment tax applies

### 6.2 Alternative Tax Elections

**S-Corporation Election (Form 2553):**
- Potential payroll tax savings
- Must file separate tax return (Form 1120S)
- Requires reasonable salary if taking distributions
- Deadline: 2 months and 15 days after start of tax year

**Partnership Election:**
- Not applicable for single-member LLC
- Would apply if adding members later

### 6.3 State Tax Considerations
**Washington State:**
- No state income tax
- B&O (Business & Occupation) tax applies
- Must register for B&O tax separately
- See separate tax guide

### 7.1 Application Timeline

**Annual Tax Return:** April 15th (or October 15th with extension)

## 9. TROUBLESHOOTING

### 9.1 Lost EIN
If you lose your EIN confirmation:
- Call IRS Business Tax Line: (800) 829-4933
- Have LLC information and responsible party SSN ready
- Request verbal confirmation or letter

### 9.2 EIN Validation
To verify an EIN is valid:
- Use IRS EIN Assistant (online)
- Call IRS for verbal confirmation
- Check against official documentation

### 9.3 Common Issues
**"Entity already has EIN" error:**
- Check if LLC already applied previously
- Verify exact LLC name spelling
- Contact IRS if error persists

**Application rejected:**
- Review all information for accuracy
- Ensure LLC is properly formed in WA State
- Verify responsible party information

## 10. SECURITY CONSIDERATIONS

### 10.1 Protecting Your EIN
- Never share EIN unless necessary for business
- Use EIN instead of SSN on business documents
- Monitor for unauthorized use
- Report suspected fraud to IRS immediately

### 10.2 Authorized Use
**Appropriate sharing:**
- Banks for account opening
- Clients for tax reporting (1099 forms)
- Payment processors
- Accountants and tax preparers
- Government agencies

**Never share with:**
- Unknown parties requesting over phone/email
- Non-business social media platforms
- Unsecured websites
- Personal use applications
