**2. Systematic Scoring System**

For each business, assign a score:

| Score | Website Status | Lead Temperature |
|-------|----------------|------------------|
| **10** | No website link at all | 🔥 HOT - Contact immediately |
| **9** | Website link broken/404 | 🔥 HOT - Contact immediately |

| **5** | Good website but no social media presence | 💛 LUKEWARM - Potential |
| **4** | Good website but poor Google reviews management | 💛 LUKEWARM - Potential |
| **3** | Good website, social presence, but no marketing | 💙 COLD - Long-term nurture |

**Action Thresholds:**
- Score 8-10: Contact within 24 hours
- Score 6-7: Contact within 48 hours
- Score 4-5: Add to nurture sequence
- Score 1-3: Skip

### Mobile-Friendly Testing Process

**For Score 6-7 prospects, test their website:**

1. **Google Mobile-Friendly Test:**
   - Go to: search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly
   - Enter their website URL
   - Screenshot results for your outreach

2. **Manual Mobile Test:**
   - View their site on your phone
   - Check for: tiny text, horizontal scrolling, buttons too small
   - Time how long it takes to find contact info
   - Try to submit a contact form

3. **Speed Test:**
   - Use PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev)
   - Screenshot poor scores (anything under 50 on mobile)

## 7. Pacific Northwest-Specific Tips

### Portuguese Business Culture

#### Communication Norms
**Relationship First:**
- Portuguese business culture values personal relationships
- Don't jump straight to business in initial communications
- Ask about their business, location, how long they've been operating
- Show genuine interest in their success

**Formal vs. Informal:**
- Start formal (Você) in initial communications
- Business owners over 40 typically prefer formal approach
- Younger entrepreneurs (under 35) often comfortable with informal (tu)
- When in doubt, stay formal until they indicate otherwise

**Time Expectations:**
- Decision-making often takes longer than Northern European standards
- Don't rush the sales process
- Multiple touchpoints expected before trust is established
- August and major holidays (especially Christmas season) slow business decisions

#### Common Objections from Portuguese SMB Owners

**"Digital marketing is for big companies"**
**Response:** "Actually, digital marketing gives small businesses an advantage. Large companies spend millions on TV ads, but you can reach your exact customers for $50/day on Facebook and Google. Your customers are searching '[your service] Seattle' right now - you just need to be there when they search."

**"Portuguese customers prefer personal recommendations"**
**Response:** "You're absolutely right - word of mouth is crucial for local businesses. But what happens when someone gets a recommendation and wants to check you out online first? A professional online presence supports those recommendations and gives people confidence to call you."

**"The internet is too expensive/complicated"**
**Response:** "It used to be, but not anymore. A professional website costs less than what you spend on one month of traditional advertising, and it works 24/7. I handle all the technical details - you just focus on serving customers."

**"We've been successful without a website for [X] years"**
**Response:** "Your business skills haven't changed - they're still excellent. But customer behavior has changed. 80% of Portuguese consumers now search online before choosing a business. You're not changing what you do, you're just making sure customers can find you."

### Language Considerations

#### Portuguese vs English Outreach

**Use Portuguese For:**
- Initial cold outreach (shows respect for local culture)
- Traditional business owners (restaurants, local services)
- Businesses serving primarily Portuguese customers
- Formal initial contact

**Use English For:**
- Tech startups and modern companies
- Businesses targeting international/tourist customers
- Young entrepreneurs (often comfortable with English)
- After they respond positively in Portuguese (many switch to English)

**Bilingual Strategy:**
- Initial outreach in Portuguese
- Follow-up materials can be in English (shows international capability)
- Website proposals should be bilingual
- Always offer to continue conversation in their preferred language

**Professional Greetings:**
- "Estimado(a) [Name]" - Formal (Dear...)
- "Caro(a) [Name]" - Slightly less formal (Dear...)

## Emergency Client Generation (Need Clients Fast)

If you need clients urgently, focus on these high-conversion activities:

### Day 1-7: Blitz Approach
- **Google Maps method:** 6 hours daily, focus only on score 9-10 prospects (no website at all)
- **Phone calls:** Call every prospect immediately, don't wait for email responses
- **Local networking:** Attend every business event possible
- **Free audits:** Offer free website/marketing audits to first 10 responders

### Day 8-14: Follow-up Intensity
- **Daily follow-up:** Contact every prospect who responded positively
- **Social proof:** Create urgency with "limited spots available"
- **Partnerships:** Activate referral partnerships with complementary businesses
- **Premium positioning:** Focus on higher-value prospects who can afford $3,500/month

### Day 15-30: Close and Deliver
- **Rapid proposals:** Same-day proposals for interested prospects
- **Flexible payment:** Offer payment plans to reduce barrier to entry
- **Over-deliver:** Exceed expectations on first few clients to generate referrals
- **Document everything:** Create case studies immediately for future use

This systematic approach should generate 8-12 clients within 90 days if executed consistently. The key is daily discipline with prospect research and outreach combined with premium positioning and consultative selling.

Focus on building relationships, not just selling services. Portuguese business culture rewards patience and relationship-building with long-term client loyalty and strong referral networks.
